Who to credit, and how to give feedback
=======================================

This document is currently maintained by Philip Brohan (philip.brohan @ metoffice.gov.uk). All criticism should be directed to him - put please **don't** send email, `raise an issue <https://github.com/oldweather/DWR/issues/new>`_ instead.

If you are suggesting an improvement or extension, it is easy to `implement it yourself <replication_and_extension.html>`_. For everything else, please `raise an issue <https://github.com/oldweather/DWR/issues/new>`_. The current list of issues raised is `here <https://github.com/oldweather/DWR/issues>`_. Please send praise as well as criticism.

|

All blame should go to the maintainer; credit is more widely distributed - all those listed below have helped. Note that appearance on this list does not mean that the person or organisation named endorses this work, agrees with any of it, or even knows of its existence.

* This document was written by Philip Brohan. He was funded by the Joint DECC and Defra Integrated Climate Programme, DECC/Defra (GA01101).
* The new 1900-1910 DWR observations came from the many contributors to the `Weather Rescue citizen science project <http://weatherrescue.org>`_, led by Ed Hawkins.
* The new 1920-1960 DWR observations came from a storm variability project led by Rob Allan and Lisa Alexander.
